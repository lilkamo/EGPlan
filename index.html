The 4 Phases

Weeks 1–2 Foundation

Learn the movement patterns. 3 sets, 12–15 reps, light resistance. Stop 2 reps shy of failure every set. Build the mind-muscle connection.

Weeks 3–4 Volume Build

Add a 4th set on all main compounds. Increase band resistance. Last set of each main exercise to 1 rep in reserve. Load starts going up.

Weeks 5–6 Intensity Up

Rep range shifts to 8–12. Heavier dumbbell introduced. Last set of hip thrust, RDL, and split squat goes to true failure.

Weeks 7–8 Peak

Highest volume and intensity. Push the last 2 sets of compounds to failure. Re-measure glutes at end of week 8 — same time, same spot.

3 sessions per week with at least 1 rest day between. Suggested: Mon / Wed / Fri or Tue / Thu / Sat.

Rep to Failure?

📊 RIR Method (Reps In Reserve)

Going to full failure every set causes form breakdown and injury risk without a spotter. Instead, use RIR:

• Weeks 1–2: Stop 2 reps before failure (2 RIR)
• Weeks 3–4: Last set = 1 RIR, other sets = 2 RIR
• Weeks 5–8: Last set of main compounds = true failure

Day A — Hip Dominant + Abs

7 exercises · ~70 min · Glute Focus

Warm-up (5–7 min)

Hip circles x 10 each direction · Cat-cow x 10 · Glute bridge hold 30s · Band walks 20 steps each way

Glute Bridge (Bodyweight)
3 × 15

Lie on back, feet flat, drive hips up hard. Hold 2 seconds at the top every rep.

💡 Squeeze your glutes like you're cracking a walnut — don't just lift your hips

Band Hip Thrust (off couch)
3 × 12

Upper back on couch edge, band across hip crease. Drive through heels, not toes. 90s rest.

💡 Tuck chin, keep ribs down. At the top your body should be a straight plank.

Romanian Deadlift (2×10 lb DBs)
3 × 12

Hinge at hips, push them back. Feel the hamstring stretch at the bottom. Drive hips forward to stand — not a squat.

💡 Run the DBs down your thighs — they should almost graze your legs

Donkey Kick (Bodyweight)
3 × 15 each

On all fours. Keep knee bent at 90°. Kick foot straight up toward ceiling. Pause at top. Lower with control. Do all reps one side, then switch.

💡 Don't arch your lower back — keep hips square to the floor throughout

Clam Shell (Band)
3 × 15 each

Band above knees. Lie on side, knees bent. Rotate top knee up like a clam. Keep feet together, hips stacked — don't roll back.

💡 This works the glute medius — critical for that rounded outer look

Dead Bug
3 × 10 each side

Lie on back, arms up, knees 90°. Lower opposite arm + leg slowly. Lower back stays pressed to floor the entire time.

Plank Hold
3 × 30 sec

Forearm plank. Brace your abs, squeeze glutes, breathe. No sagging hips, no hiking the butt up.

Day B — Knee Dominant + Abs

7 exercises · ~70 min · Glute Focus

Warm-up (5–7 min)

Leg swings x 10 each · Bodyweight squat x 15 slow · Lateral band walks 15 steps each · Ankle rolls

Goblet Squat (10 lb DB)
3 × 15

Hold one DB at chest. Feet shoulder-width, toes slightly out. Sit back and down — chest tall. Drive through full foot back up.

💡 Think "spread the floor" with your feet as you stand — activates glutes harder

Bulgarian Split Squat (DB)
3 × 10 each

Rear foot on couch, front foot far enough forward that shin stays vertical. Lower slowly (3 sec down). Drive through front heel up.

💡 Hardest exercise in this program — if form breaks, drop the weight immediately

Reverse Lunge (DBs)
3 × 12 each

Step back and lower. Front knee stays behind toes. Keep front heel grounded. Step feet back together. Alternate or do all one side.

Fire Hydrant (Bodyweight)
3 × 15 each

On all fours. Keeping knee bent 90°, lift leg out to the side — like a dog at a fire hydrant. Keep hips level — don't tilt. Slow lower.

💡 Works the upper glute medius — targets the area above the hip for that shelf look

Band Side Step
3 × 15 each way

Band above knees, slight squat position. Step out wide, then bring feet together. Stay low the whole time. Don't let knees collapse in.

Bicycle Crunch
3 × 20 total

Slow and controlled. Fully extend the straight leg low. Rotate your elbow toward the opposite knee — not just your head.

Leg Raise
3 × 12

Lie flat. Lower back pressed to floor. Raise both legs to 90°, lower slowly without touching floor. Keep core braced throughout.

8 exercises · ~75 min · Glute Focus

Warm-up (5–7 min)

Hip thrusts BW x 20 slow · Clam shells x 15 each · Donkey kicks x 10 each · Cat-cow x 10

Hip Thrust (DB on hips)
3 × 12

Both DBs resting on hip crease. Upper back on couch. Drive up — hold 1 second at top. This is your main compound — give it everything.

💡 Use a folded towel under the DBs if they dig in at the hip crease

Sumo Squat (DB)
3 × 15

Wide stance, toes pointed out at 45°. Hold one DB vertically at chest. Squat deep — feel inner thigh and glute stretch.

Donkey Kick + Band
3 × 15 each

Add a band around ankle/foot this week if available. Same form — kick up and pause. The band adds resistance at the top range where glutes are strongest.

💡 Squeeze for a full 1-second count at the top before lowering

Fire Hydrant + Band
3 × 15 each

Band above knee. Lift out and slightly back — not straight to the side. Imagine drawing a wide arc with your knee. Full range, controlled lower.

Good Morning (BW)
3 × 12

Hands behind head or across chest. Hinge forward from hips, soft knee bend. Feel the posterior chain stretch. Squeeze glutes to stand back up.

Frog Pump
3 × 20

Lie on back, soles of feet together in a diamond shape. Pump hips up and down. Short range, high reps, constant glute tension. Feels intense fast.

💡 Great glute burnout — don't skip this one even when tired

Russian Twist (DB)
3 × 15 each

Sit back at 45°. Hold one DB. Rotate side to side — tap DB to floor each time. Feet can be planted or lifted for more challenge.

Hollow Body Hold
3 × 25 sec

Lie flat. Arms overhead. Raise arms, legs, and shoulders off floor. Press lower back down — the whole body forms a gentle hollow. Breathe steadily.

Why a slight surplus?

At 100 lbs / 5'2", maintenance is roughly 1,600–1,750 cal/day with 3x weekly training. To actually build glute muscle you need to eat slightly above that. Cutting while trying to grow glutes = very slow results. A 200–300 calorie surplus is the sweet spot.

Protein Tips

Hit 110g protein daily — this is non-negotiable

Protein is the direct building block for glute muscle. Spread it across 3–4 meals with 25–35g per meal. Best sources: chicken breast, eggs, Greek yogurt, cottage cheese, salmon, tuna, lean beef, protein shakes.

Don't fear carbs

Carbs fuel the workouts and replenish muscle glycogen for recovery. Rice, oats, sweet potato, fruit, whole wheat bread — all great options. Eat more carbs on training days, slightly fewer on rest days if you want to be precise.

Creatine Monohydrate — 3–5g daily

The single most researched and effective supplement available. Mix into any drink, any time of day. Takes 2–3 weeks to fully saturate. Increases strength, power, and muscle volume. Completely safe. No loading phase needed — just 3–5g every single day consistently.

Hydration

Aim for at least 80–100 oz of water daily. Dehydration shows up as reduced strength and poor muscle pump. Add electrolytes (a pinch of salt, some fruit) after sweaty sessions.

How to Measure Correctly

📏 Glute Circumference

Measure at the widest point of the glutes — usually a few inches below the waist. Tape measure should be parallel to the floor. Measure relaxed (not flexed). Always same time of day (morning is most consistent). Same spot every time.

📸 Progress Photos

Same lighting, same angle (front, side, back), same time of day, every 2 weeks. Photos reveal changes that the scale and tape miss — particularly shape and composition shifts.

⚖️ Scale Weight

Expect slow gain — 0.5–1 lb per month is ideal for lean muscle gain at her stats. Scale will fluctuate daily with water and food — track weekly averages, not single weigh-ins. Weigh morning after bathroom, before eating.

📓 Workout Log

Log every session: exercise, sets, reps, weight used. A set that's harder this week than last week = you're progressing. If the numbers aren't going up after 3+ sessions, something needs to change (usually nutrition or sleep).

Progressive Overload Rules

When to increase weight or resistance

When she can complete ALL sets with 2+ reps to spare comfortably, it's time to go heavier. Don't add reps indefinitely — go up in load. On hip thrust, expect to increase resistance every 2–3 weeks minimum if nutrition is right.

How to increase when you don't have heavier weights

Slow the tempo (3-second lower). Add a pause at the hardest point. Use heavier band. Reduce rest time. Add reps. Superset two exercises. All of these increase difficulty without needing more weight.

Red Flags to Watch

⚠️ Lower back pain during RDL or hip thrust

Stop the exercise. Likely cause: rounding the spine (RDL) or hyperextending at the top (hip thrust). Lower the weight, review form. Pain ≠ effort — form breaks first.

⚠️ Knees caving inward on squats

Reduce weight immediately. Add band above knees to cue abductors. This puts heavy stress on the knee joint and must be corrected before loading up.

⚠️ No strength gain by week 4

Almost always a nutrition issue. Check calories — likely undereating and not recovering between sessions. Increase food intake, especially protein and carbs.

⚠️ Feeling flat and fatigued every session

If 3+ sessions in a row feel terrible, take a deload week — train at 50% volume with light loads. This is a feature, not a failure. Recovery is when muscle actually grows.

⚠️ No glute activation / feeling it in quads only

Mind-muscle connection needs work. Before each exercise, do 10 slow BW glute bridges squeezing hard at the top. Pre-activating the glute helps your brain recruit it during heavier movements.

Sleep & Recovery

😴 Sleep is where muscle is built

Aim for 7–9 hours. Muscle is not built during the workout — it's built during sleep when growth hormone is released. Poor sleep = poor gains, regardless of how hard she trains. Non-negotiable.

🚶 Active rest days

Walk, stretch, yoga, or light mobility on rest days. Complete inactivity stiffens up muscles. 20–30 minutes of easy movement improves recovery and reduces soreness for the next session.
