The 4 Phases

Weeks 1–2 Foundation

Learn the movement patterns. 3 sets, 12–15 reps, light resistance. Stop 2 reps shy of failure every set. Build the mind-muscle connection.

Weeks 3–4 Volume Build

Add a 4th set on all main compounds. Increase band resistance. Last set of each main exercise to 1 rep in reserve.

Weeks 5–6 Intensity Up

Rep range shifts to 8–12. Heavier dumbbell introduced. Last set of hip thrust, RDL, and split squat goes to true failure.

Weeks 7–8 Peak

Highest volume and intensity. Push the last 2 sets of compounds to failure. Re-measure glutes at end of week 8.

Training Structure

📅 Schedule

3 sessions per week with at least 1 rest day between. Suggested: Mon / Wed / Fri or Tue / Thu / Sat

Day A — Hip dominant glutes + Abs
Day B — Knee dominant glutes + Abs
Day C — Full glute circuit + Abs finisher

Rep to Failure?

📊 RIR Method (Reps In Reserve)

Going to full failure every set causes form breakdown without a spotter. Use RIR instead:

• Weeks 1–2: Stop 2 reps before failure (2 RIR)
• Weeks 3–4: Last set = 1 RIR, others = 2 RIR
• Weeks 5–8: Last set of main compounds = true failure

Day A — Hip Dominant + Abs

7 exercises · ~70 min · Glute Focus

Warm-up (5–7 min)

Hip circles x10 each · Cat-cow x10 · Glute bridge hold 30s · Band walks 20 steps each way

Glute Bridge (Bodyweight)
3 × 15

Lie on back, feet flat, drive hips up hard. Hold 2 seconds at the top every rep.

💡 Squeeze like you're cracking a walnut — don't just lift the hips

Band Hip Thrust (off couch)
3 × 12

Upper back on couch edge, band across hip crease. Drive through heels, not toes. 90s rest.

💡 Chin tucked, ribs down. At the top the body should form a straight plank.

Romanian Deadlift (2×10 lb DBs)
3 × 12

Hinge at hips, push them back. Feel the hamstring stretch at the bottom. Drive hips forward to stand.

💡 Run DBs down the thighs — they should almost graze the legs

Donkey Kick (Bodyweight)
3 × 15 each

On all fours. Keep knee bent 90°. Kick foot straight up toward ceiling. Pause at top. Lower with control.

💡 Don't arch the lower back — keep hips square to the floor throughout

Fire Hydrant (Bodyweight)
3 × 15 each

On all fours. Keeping knee bent 90°, lift leg out to the side. Keep hips level — don't tilt. Slow lower.

💡 Works the upper glute medius — targets the area above the hip for that shelf look

Dead Bug
3 × 10 each

Arms up, knees 90°. Lower opposite arm + leg slowly. Lower back stays pressed to floor.

Plank Hold
3 × 30 sec

Forearm plank. Brace abs, squeeze glutes, breathe. No sagging hips.

Day B — Knee Dominant + Abs

7 exercises · ~70 min · Glute Focus

Warm-up (5–7 min)

Leg swings x10 each · BW squat x15 slow · Lateral band walks 15 steps each · Ankle rolls

Goblet Squat (10 lb DB)
3 × 15

Hold one DB at chest. Feet shoulder-width, toes slightly out. Sit back and down — chest tall.

💡 Think "spread the floor" with the feet as you stand — activates glutes harder

Bulgarian Split Squat (DB)
3 × 10 each

Rear foot on couch, front foot far forward. Lower slowly (3 sec down). Drive through front heel up.

💡 Hardest exercise in this program — if form breaks, drop the weight

Reverse Lunge (DBs)
3 × 12 each

Step back and lower. Front knee behind toes. Front heel grounded. Step feet back together.

Fire Hydrant (Bodyweight)
3 × 15 each

On all fours. Lift leg out to the side — like a dog at a fire hydrant. Keep hips level. Slow lower.

Band Side Step
3 × 15 each way

Band above knees, slight squat position. Step out wide, then bring feet together. Stay low throughout.

Bicycle Crunch
3 × 20 total

Slow and controlled. Fully extend the straight leg low. Rotate elbow toward opposite knee.

Leg Raise
3 × 12

Lower back pressed to floor. Raise both legs to 90°, lower slowly without touching floor.

Day C — Full Glute + Abs Finisher

8 exercises · ~75 min · Glute Focus

Warm-up (5–7 min)

Hip thrusts BW x20 slow · Clam shells x15 each · Donkey kicks x10 each · Cat-cow x10

Hip Thrust (DBs on hips)
3 × 12

Both DBs on hip crease. Upper back on couch. Drive up — hold 1 sec at top. Main compound.

💡 Use a folded towel under the DBs if they dig into the hip crease

Sumo Squat (DB)
3 × 15

Wide stance, toes 45°. Hold one DB vertically at chest. Squat deep — feel inner thigh and glute stretch.

Donkey Kick + Band
3 × 15 each

Loop band around ankle if available. Same form — kick up and pause 1 sec at top.

💡 Band adds resistance at the top range where glutes are strongest

Fire Hydrant + Band
3 × 15 each

Band above knee. Lift out and slightly back — not straight to the side. Draw a wide arc with the knee.

Good Morning (BW)
3 × 12

Hands behind head. Hinge forward from hips, soft knee bend. Squeeze glutes to stand back up.

Frog Pump
3 × 20

Lie on back, soles of feet together. Pump hips up and down. Short range, high reps, constant glute tension.

💡 Great burnout — don't skip even when tired

Russian Twist (DB)
3 × 15 each

Sit back at 45°. Hold one DB. Rotate side to side — tap DB to floor each time.

Hollow Body Hold
3 × 25 sec

Arms overhead. Raise arms, legs, and shoulders off floor. Press lower back down. Breathe steadily.

Why a slight surplus?

At 100 lbs / 5'2", maintenance is roughly 1,600–1,750 cal/day with 3× weekly training. To actually build glute muscle you need to eat slightly above that. Cutting while trying to grow glutes = very slow results. A 200–300 calorie surplus is the sweet spot — enough to build without gaining excess fat.

No-meat months

On no-meat months, prioritise: eggs, Greek yogurt, cottage cheese, tofu, tempeh, lentils, chickpeas, edamame, quinoa (complete protein), and protein powder. The goal is still 110g protein daily — it just takes more planning without meat. The Meal Ideas tab has options for both.

Creatine monohydrate — 3–5g daily

The single most evidence-backed supplement available. Mix into any drink, any time of day. Takes 2–3 weeks to fully load. Increases strength, power, and muscle volume. Completely safe, no loading phase needed. Works equally well on no-meat months.

Hydration

Aim for at least 80–100 oz of water daily. Dehydration shows up as reduced strength and poor muscle pump. Add electrolytes (pinch of salt, some fruit juice) after sweaty sessions.

Protein Tips

Hit 110g protein daily — non-negotiable

Protein is the direct building block for glute muscle. Spread across 3–4 meals with 25–35g per meal. Best meat sources: chicken breast, eggs, salmon, tuna, lean beef, Greek yogurt, cottage cheese. Best no-meat sources: tofu, tempeh, edamame, lentils, chickpeas, cottage cheese, eggs, Greek yogurt, protein powder.

Don't fear carbs

Carbs fuel workouts and replenish muscle glycogen for recovery. Rice, oats, sweet potato, fruit, whole wheat bread — all great. Eat more carbs on training days, slightly less on rest days.

Protein timing

Try to get 25–35g protein within 1–2 hours after training. This doesn't have to be a shake — any high-protein meal counts. Consistency across the whole day matters more than timing.

Band Upgrade — Recommended

Additional Resistance Bands (Loop Set) GET SOON

~$15–25 for a full set

Having light, medium, heavy, and extra-heavy bands is key for progressive overload on donkey kicks, fire hydrants, clam shells, hip thrusts, and side steps. If the current bands are light/medium, a heavy and extra-heavy will be needed by weeks 3–4. If current bands are heavy, grab a light set for warm-ups and isolation work.

Exercise Mat MUST HAVE

~$15–20

Heavier Dumbbell(s) — 20–25 lb BY WEEK 5

~$25–50 single / $60–120 pair

Progress will stall on hip thrusts and RDLs without heavier weight. A single 25 lb DB covers hip thrusts well. A pair opens up more bilateral movements.

~$20–35

Adds meaningful resistance to donkey kicks and fire hydrants without needing a band anchor. Great for increasing intensity on floor exercises that drive glute medius growth.

How to Measure Correctly

📏 Glute Circumference

Measure at the widest point of the glutes. Tape parallel to the floor. Measure relaxed (not flexed). Always same time of day — morning is most consistent. Same spot every time.

📸 Progress Photos

Same lighting, angle (front, side, back), time of day, every 2 weeks. Photos reveal changes that scale and tape miss — particularly shape shifts.

⚖️ Scale Weight

Expect slow gain — 0.5–1 lb per month is ideal for lean muscle. Scale fluctuates daily with water and food. Track weekly averages. Weigh morning after bathroom, before eating.

📓 Workout Log

Log every session: exercise, sets, reps, weight used. Numbers going up = progress. If they stall for 3+ sessions, something needs to change (usually nutrition or sleep).

Progressive Overload Rules

When to increase load

When completing all sets with 2+ reps to spare comfortably, go heavier. Don't add reps indefinitely — go up in resistance. On hip thrust, expect to increase every 2–3 weeks minimum if nutrition is right.

When you don't have heavier weights

Slow the tempo (3-sec lower). Add a pause at the hardest point. Use heavier band. Reduce rest time. Add reps. Superset two exercises. All increase difficulty without needing more weight.

Red Flags to Watch

⚠️ Lower back pain during RDL or hip thrust

Stop the exercise. Likely cause: rounding spine (RDL) or hyperextending at top (hip thrust). Lower weight, review form. Pain ≠ effort.

⚠️ Knees caving inward on squats

Reduce weight immediately. Add band above knees to cue abductors. Heavy stress on the knee joint — correct before loading up.

⚠️ No strength gain by week 4

Almost always nutrition. Check calories — likely undereating and not recovering. Increase food, especially protein and carbs.

⚠️ Feeling flat and fatigued every session

If 3+ sessions feel terrible, take a deload week — 50% volume, light loads. Recovery is when muscle actually grows.

⚠️ No glute activation — feeling it in quads only

Do 10 slow BW glute bridges squeezing hard at top before each exercise. Pre-activating the glute helps the brain recruit it during heavier movements.

Sleep & Recovery

😴 Sleep is where muscle is built

Aim for 7–9 hours. Muscle is built during sleep when growth hormone is released. Poor sleep = poor gains regardless of training effort. Non-negotiable.

🚶 Active rest days

Walk, stretch, yoga, or light mobility on rest days. 20–30 minutes of easy movement improves recovery and reduces soreness for the next session.
